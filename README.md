## Description

Compiled database on public mental health spending in 21 Ibero-American countries (Argentina, Bolivia, Brazil, Chile, Colombia, Costa Rica, Cuba, Ecuador, El Salvador, Spain, Guatemala, Honduras, Mexico, Nicaragua, Panama, Paraguay, Peru, Portugal, Dominican Republic, Uruguay, and Venezuela) for the period 2000–2026.

The database includes 8 categories of analysis:
1. Percentage of total health spending allocated to mental health (annual, 2000–2026)
2. Per capita spending on mental health in USD (annual, 2000–2026)
3. Mental health spending as a percentage of GDP (annual, 2000–2026)
4. Total health spending as a percentage of GDP — context (World Bank, annual, 2000–2026)
5. Hospital vs. community distribution of mental health spending (reference years)
6. Confirmed milestones and specific data by country with official sources
7. Legal framework and national mental health policies by country
8. Comparison with WHO recommendations and regional ranking 2023–2024

Main sources: WHO Mental Health Atlas (2001, 2005, 2011, 2014, 2017, 2020, 2024 editions); PAHO "Nueva Agenda para la Salud Mental en las Américas" (2023); World Bank World Development Indicators; CIEP México; National Ministries of Health; FIFARMA 2023; BinaSS Costa Rica.
